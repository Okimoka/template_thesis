This thesis extends the Healthy Brain Network (HBN) EEG resource with eye-tracking data and evaluates whether it supports large-scale fixation-related potential (FRP) analysis. All 11 HBN-EEG releases were merged into a single BIDS-compatible dataset, the original eye-tracking files were linked to their EEG recordings, and the preprocessing workflow was extended to parse SMI text exports and quantify synchronization quality for automatic curation. This yielded 3784 well-synchronized free-view recordings and enabled reproducible combined EEG and eye-tracking preprocessing. The curated data were then used for an exploratory FRP analysis of the movie-viewing paradigms. While synchronization quality clearly mattered and naive FRPs showed meaningful structure, the deconvolved FRPs did not match the canonical pattern reported in controlled studies, suggesting that residual ocular artifacts or unresolved methodological issues still limit interpretation. Overall, the thesis makes HBN more usable for future combined EEG and eye-tracking research.
